Venetian Crab Linguine

Crab linguine is a traditional Venetian dish comprised largely of linguine and chunks of crab meat. With just a few good quality ingredients, this crab pasta is bursting with flavour and very easy to make at home.

Ingredients

  • 300 g good quality dried linguine (10.5oz)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large clove garlic, crushed
  • ½ cup dry white wine (125ml)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, chopped in half
  • 1 tablespoon cream (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt flakes
  • ½ teaspoon freshly cracked pepper
  • ¾ cup cooked crab meat

Steps

  1. Place a saucepan of salted water over high heat and bring to the boil, then add your pasta.
  2. Place the oil and garlic to a frying pan over medium heat, stirring. Once the oil is hot, saute the garlic for about 1 minute but don’t let it get too dark.
  3. Add the wine and turn the heat up to high. Let it bubble away for 2-3 minutes until the wine has almost totally evaporated.
  4. Add the cherry tomatoes and saute for about 2 minutes, then add the salt and pepper.
  5. Add 1/2 a cup of the pasta water followed by the cream and give it a good stir. Add the crab and stir until heated through (only about 30 seconds).
  6. By now the pasta should be al dente, so drain the water away and add the pasta to the sauce. Give it all a good mix, check the seasoning and serve.
